Common Tarot Mistakes for Beginners

Learning tarot takes time, and mistakes are a natural part of the process. Most beginner difficulties come from trying to learn too much at once, asking unclear questions or expecting every reading to provide a perfect answer.

⚠

Trying to Memorise Every Card Immediately

A tarot deck contains 78 cards, and each card can have several meanings depending on the question, position and surrounding cards.

Trying to memorise everything at once can make tarot feel overwhelming.

Focus first on the main theme of each card. Your understanding will become more detailed through regular practice.

📖

Relying Only on the Guidebook

Guidebooks are useful, but they should not replace your own observation.

Before reading the official meaning, look at the card and notice:

  • The colours
  • The expressions
  • The setting
  • The direction of movement
  • The symbols that stand out
  • The feeling created by the image

Your first impression can add important meaning to the traditional interpretation.

?

Asking Questions That Are Too Vague

A question such as “What will happen?” gives the reading very little direction.

A clearer question might be:

“What should I understand about my current work situation?”

Clear questions make the cards easier to interpret and help keep the reading focused.

↻

Repeating the Same Question

Drawing more cards because you dislike the first answer can quickly make a reading confusing.

Instead of asking the same question again, write down the original reading and give yourself time to reflect.

You can return to the subject later if the circumstances genuinely change.

10

Using Too Many Cards

Large spreads can look impressive, but they are often difficult for beginners to interpret.

Start with one-card or three-card readings. These provide enough information to practise without creating unnecessary confusion.

Add more cards only when each position has a clear purpose.

✦

Reading Every Card Separately

In a spread, the cards should work together as one message.

After interpreting each card, look for connections:

  • Repeated suits
  • Repeated numbers
  • Similar colours
  • People facing towards or away from each other
  • A movement from difficulty towards resolution
  • Several cards sharing the same theme
☾

Treating Tarot as a Fixed Prediction

Tarot is most useful as a tool for reflection, perspective and exploring possible directions.

A reading does not remove personal choice or prove that one outcome must happen.

Circumstances can change, and your actions can influence what happens next.

⚖

Asking Tarot to Make Every Decision

Tarot can help you consider a choice, but it should not replace your judgement.

Instead of asking:

“Should I do this?”

Try asking:

“What should I consider before making this decision?”

This keeps responsibility for the final choice with you.

👁

Ignoring the Question or Card Position

A card does not have exactly the same meaning in every reading.

The interpretation should relate to the question and the position it occupies.

For example, The Hermit in a “challenge” position may suggest isolation, while in an “advice” position it may encourage reflection and quiet study.

↓

Feeling Forced to Use Reversed Cards

Beginners sometimes believe that reversed cards must be included from the beginning.

They are optional. You can learn upright meanings first and introduce reversals later.

Even without reversed cards, challenges and difficult themes can still appear through the question, position and surrounding cards.

☠

Assuming Difficult Cards Are Always Negative

Cards such as Death, The Tower, The Devil and the Ten of Swords can feel frightening.

However, these cards often represent change, release, truth, endings or the need to recognise an unhealthy pattern.

Look at the full context before deciding that a card predicts something terrible.

♡

Reading While Extremely Upset

Strong emotions can make it difficult to interpret cards calmly.

You may focus only on meanings that confirm your fears or hopes.

Take a break, write down the question and return when you feel more settled.

🔍

Searching Until You Find the Meaning You Want

Looking at many websites and guidebooks can create more confusion, especially when each source uses different wording.

Choose one or two trusted references while learning.

Compare the meanings with the artwork and the question rather than searching for the most comforting answer.

✎

Not Recording Your Readings

It is easy to forget the original question or change your interpretation after the event.

Recording a reading helps you review your progress honestly.

Write down:

  • The date
  • The question
  • The spread used
  • The cards drawn
  • Your first interpretation
  • What became clearer later
🔮

Buying Too Many Decks Too Quickly

Different decks use different imagery, titles and interpretations.

Constantly switching decks can make learning harder.

Spend time with one beginner-friendly deck before collecting several different styles.

✧

Expecting Every Reading to Feel Clear

Some readings make sense immediately. Others need time and reflection.

An unclear reading does not mean you have failed.

Record what you notice, avoid drawing unnecessary extra cards and review the reading later.

✓

A Better Beginner Approach

  1. Ask one clear question.
  2. Use a simple spread.
  3. Study the imagery before checking meanings.
  4. Interpret each card in its position.
  5. Connect the cards into one message.
  6. Write the reading down.
  7. Avoid repeating the question immediately.
  8. Allow your understanding to develop over time.

Remember

You do not need to read perfectly. Keep your questions clear, your spreads simple and your interpretations open. Confidence grows through patient and regular practice.

Practise with one clear question and a simple spread.
