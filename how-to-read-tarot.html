Tarot for beginners

How to Read Tarot Cards: A Beginner's Guide

Learning to read tarot does not require special powers, perfect intuition or years of experience. It begins with curiosity, patience and a willingness to reflect on what the cards may reveal about your thoughts, feelings and circumstances.

☾

What Is Tarot?

Tarot is a system of illustrated cards commonly used for reflection, storytelling and personal insight. A traditional tarot deck contains 78 cards, with each card representing different experiences, emotions, challenges and possibilities.

Some people use tarot as part of a spiritual practice, while others simply use it as a way to organise their thoughts and look at a situation from a different perspective.

Tarot does not need to be treated as a fixed prediction of the future. A reading can instead act as a conversation with yourself. The images and meanings of the cards can encourage you to consider things you may have overlooked.

✦

Understanding the Tarot Deck

A standard tarot deck is divided into two main groups: the Major Arcana and the Minor Arcana.

The Major Arcana

The Major Arcana contains 22 cards. These include well-known cards such as The Fool, The Magician, The Lovers, The Hermit, Death, The Star and The World.

Major Arcana cards often represent important life lessons, turning points, personal growth or larger themes affecting your situation.

The Minor Arcana

The Minor Arcana contains 56 cards divided into four suits: Cups, Pentacles, Swords and Wands. These cards often relate to everyday experiences, relationships, decisions, work, emotions and personal challenges.

  • Cups often relate to emotions, relationships, intuition and connection.
  • Pentacles often relate to work, money, health, stability and practical matters.
  • Swords often relate to thoughts, communication, conflict and decision-making.
  • Wands often relate to creativity, motivation, passion and action.
✧

Prepare for Your Reading

You do not need candles, crystals or a complicated ritual to read tarot. A calm environment can help, but the most important thing is giving yourself enough time and attention to think clearly.

Before beginning, sit comfortably and take a few slow breaths. Think about why you are consulting the cards and what you would like to understand.

Try to approach the reading with an open mind. Avoid deciding in advance what you want the cards to say. A useful reading may sometimes highlight something unexpected.

Ask a Meaningful Question

The question you ask can shape the usefulness of the reading. Open questions usually encourage more reflection than questions that can only be answered with yes or no.

Instead of asking:

“Will I get the job?”

You might ask:

“What should I understand about this job opportunity?”

Other helpful questions include:

  • What should I focus on today?
  • What can I learn from this situation?
  • What may be preventing me from moving forward?
  • How can I approach this relationship more thoughtfully?
  • What do I need to understand about my current path?

Questions centred on your own choices and actions are usually more helpful than questions attempting to uncover another person's private thoughts.

Shuffle and Choose Your Cards

There is no single correct way to shuffle a tarot deck. You can shuffle it like ordinary playing cards, mix the cards gently on a table or divide the deck into smaller piles before putting it together again.

While shuffling, keep your question in mind. When you feel ready, stop and choose your card or cards.

Some readers take the top card. Others spread the deck across a table and choose whichever card attracts their attention. Both methods are valid.

When using the free reading tool on this website, take the same approach: pause, focus on your question and select the card that feels right.

👁

Look at the Card Before Reading Its Meaning

Before looking up the traditional meaning, spend a moment observing the artwork.

Notice the people, objects, colours, expressions and surroundings shown on the card. Think about the atmosphere of the image.

Ask yourself:

  • What did I notice first?
  • What emotion does this image create?
  • Does the card feel peaceful, active, uncertain or challenging?
  • Is there a symbol that reminds me of my current situation?
  • What might the person in the card be thinking or experiencing?

Your first reaction can be an important part of the reading. It may reveal how the card connects with your own experience.

✨

Combine Traditional Meaning with Intuition

Every tarot card has traditional meanings, but those meanings are not rigid definitions. The same card can offer different guidance depending on the question, position and surrounding cards.

Begin with the recognised meaning of the card. Then consider how that meaning might apply to your situation.

For example, The Hermit is often connected with solitude, reflection and inner guidance. In one reading, it could suggest taking time alone before making a decision. In another, it could highlight the need to seek wisdom or step away from outside pressure.

A strong interpretation combines three things: the traditional meaning, the imagery on the card and your personal response to it.

↕

Upright and Reversed Cards

Some tarot readers interpret cards differently when they appear upside down. These are known as reversed cards.

A reversed card does not automatically mean something bad. It may suggest that the card's energy is blocked, delayed, internalised or expressed in an unhelpful way.

Beginners do not need to use reversed meanings immediately. You can keep every card upright while learning the basic structure of the deck.

Once you feel comfortable, you can introduce reversals gradually. There is no rule requiring you to use them.

Try a One-Card Reading

A one-card reading is one of the best ways to practise. It keeps the message clear and allows you to concentrate fully on one image and meaning.

You might draw one card for:

  • The energy of the day
  • Something to remember
  • A lesson to consider
  • Guidance about one situation
  • A quality you may need to develop

Write down the card, your first impression and how you think it relates to your question. Reviewing your notes later can help you recognise how your understanding develops.

3

Try a Three-Card Spread

When you feel comfortable with one card, try drawing three. Each card is assigned a position that gives it a role in the reading.

A simple three-card spread could represent:

  • Card one: the current situation
  • Card two: the challenge or influence
  • Card three: advice or a possible next step

You could also use:

  • Past, present and future
  • Mind, body and spirit
  • Situation, action and outcome
  • What to keep, what to release and what to welcome

Read each card individually before considering how the three cards connect. Look for repeated symbols, similar themes or contrasting messages.

✎

Keep a Tarot Journal

Recording your readings is one of the most useful ways to learn. A tarot journal does not need to be complicated.

For each reading, write down:

  • The date
  • Your question
  • The card or cards you drew
  • Your first impressions
  • The traditional meanings
  • How the reading may relate to your life
  • Any thoughts you have when reviewing it later

Over time, you may notice recurring cards or themes. You may also discover that your personal connection with a card becomes deeper than its short description in a guidebook.

⚠

Common Beginner Mistakes

Drawing Too Many Cards

Drawing card after card because you dislike or do not understand the first answer can make a reading confusing. Start with one or three cards and spend time reflecting on them.

Asking the Same Question Repeatedly

Repeating the same reading until you receive the answer you hoped for can reduce its usefulness. Give yourself time to consider the original message.

Treating Every Card as a Prediction

Cards can describe feelings, influences, possibilities and advice. They do not always describe a future event.

Believing Difficult Cards Are Always Negative

Cards such as Death, The Tower or Ten of Swords can appear frightening, but they often represent change, endings, release or the need to rebuild. Context matters.

Ignoring Your Own Judgement

Tarot should support your thinking, not replace it. You remain responsible for your choices and actions.

♢

Do You Need to Memorise Every Card?

You do not need to memorise all 78 cards before beginning. Learning happens naturally through regular practice.

Start by understanding the broad themes of the Major Arcana and the four Minor Arcana suits. Keep a guide nearby and look up cards as you draw them.

With time, the meanings will become familiar. More importantly, you will begin to recognise how each card changes depending on the question and surrounding cards.

☼

How Often Should You Read Tarot?

There is no fixed limit, but it can be helpful to avoid relying on tarot for every small decision.

A daily one-card reading can be a peaceful reflective habit. For larger questions, consider waiting until the situation changes or you have had time to act on the previous reading.

Tarot is most helpful when it encourages thought, awareness and constructive action rather than anxiety or dependence.

♡

Trust Your Own Perspective

Different tarot readers may interpret the same card differently. This does not necessarily mean one interpretation is wrong.

Tarot is symbolic. The meaning comes from the relationship between the card, the question and the person reading it.

Allow your understanding to grow naturally. You do not need to force an interpretation or make every detail fit perfectly.

Sometimes the most useful response is simply: “I need more time to think about this card.”

Remember

Tarot readings are best used for reflection, inspiration and entertainment. They should not replace professional medical, legal, financial or mental health advice.

Your choices, actions and circumstances continue to shape what happens next.

Take a quiet moment, think about your question and practise what you have learned.
