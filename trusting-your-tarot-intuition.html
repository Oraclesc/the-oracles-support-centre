How to Trust Your Intuition When Reading Tarot

Intuition is not about guessing or possessing a mysterious ability. In tarot, it means noticing your first impressions, recognising meaningful details and allowing the imagery to speak alongside the traditional meanings of the cards.

👁

What Does Intuition Mean in Tarot?

Intuition is the immediate understanding or feeling that appears before you begin analysing everything logically.

When you look at a tarot card, one symbol, colour, character or emotion may stand out before anything else.

That first observation can help you understand which part of the card is most relevant to the question.

📖

Intuition Does Not Replace Card Knowledge

Traditional meanings provide the foundation of a tarot reading.

Intuition helps you decide how that meaning applies to the specific question and circumstances.

The strongest readings usually combine both: knowledge of the card and careful attention to what stands out in the moment.

✦

Begin With What You Notice

Before opening a guidebook, spend a few moments looking at the card.

Ask yourself:

  • Which part of the image caught my attention first?
  • What emotion does the card create?
  • Does the image feel active or still?
  • Does the card seem welcoming, tense or uncertain?
  • What might the character in the card be thinking?
🎨

Pay Attention to Colour

Colours can influence the atmosphere of a card.

Bright colours may suggest confidence, openness or activity. Darker colours may create a quieter, uncertain or reflective feeling.

There is no need to memorise a fixed meaning for every colour. Notice how the colours affect you in that particular reading.

🙂

Study Expressions and Body Language

The figures within tarot cards often reveal more through their posture and expressions than through memorised keywords.

Consider whether a person appears confident, guarded, distracted, relaxed or afraid.

Notice whether they are moving forward, looking backwards or turning away from another figure.

→

Notice Movement and Direction

Ask where the energy of the card appears to be moving.

A figure moving towards another card may suggest engagement, progress or confrontation.

A figure facing away may suggest avoidance, independence or leaving something behind.

Direction becomes especially useful when reading several cards together.

💭

Describe the Card Before Interpreting It

When you feel unsure, describe exactly what you see without trying to give it a tarot meaning.

For example:

“A person is standing in front of several choices and cannot clearly see what each one contains.”

This simple description of the Seven of Cups already suggests choice, uncertainty, imagination and the need for clarity.

Keep the Question in Mind

Intuition works best when it remains connected to the question.

The same card can highlight different details in readings about work, relationships or personal development.

Ask which part of the image relates most directly to the subject being explored.

⚖

Intuition or Personal Bias?

It can be difficult to separate intuition from fear, hope or personal preference.

Intuition is often quiet and straightforward. Bias tends to repeat the answer you desperately want or fear.

Ask yourself whether your interpretation is genuinely supported by the card, the question and the surrounding cards.

♡

Be Careful When Emotions Are Strong

Strong emotions can make every card appear to confirm the same worry.

When you are extremely upset, frightened or desperate for a particular answer, it may be better to pause the reading.

Return when you feel calmer, or ask a more reflective question about what you can understand or control.

✎

Record Your First Impression

Write down your first thought before checking a guidebook or searching online.

You can then compare it with the traditional meaning and see where they connect.

Over time, a tarot journal will show which intuitive observations were useful and which were influenced by temporary emotions.

🔍

Do Not Search for Endless Meanings

Looking through many websites for the perfect interpretation can weaken your confidence.

Different sources often use different words for the same basic theme.

Choose one trusted reference, compare it with the image and then decide what fits the reading most naturally.

☾

Give Yourself a Quiet Moment

You do not need an elaborate ritual, but it can help to slow down before drawing cards.

Take a breath, read the question again and remove unnecessary distractions.

A brief pause creates space to observe the cards rather than rushing immediately towards a memorised answer.

🃏

Practise With One Card

One-card readings are an excellent way to develop intuition without becoming overwhelmed.

Draw one card and spend a minute describing the scene before reading its meaning.

Then write one sentence explaining how the card relates to your question or your day.

3

Practise With Three Cards

Once one-card readings feel comfortable, draw three cards and look for a visual story.

Notice whether the atmosphere becomes lighter or darker from left to right.

Look at whether the figures face one another and whether symbols or colours repeat across the spread.

✨

Example: The Hermit

The traditional meaning of The Hermit includes reflection, solitude, wisdom and inner guidance.

In one reading, the lantern may stand out and suggest finding enough clarity to take the next small step.

In another, the isolated figure may feel more important and suggest withdrawing too far from support.

Both interpretations remain connected to the traditional meaning, but the question determines which detail matters most.

⚠

Common Intuition Mistakes

  • Ignoring traditional meanings completely.
  • Treating every random thought as a message.
  • Changing the interpretation to get the answer you want.
  • Reading while extremely emotional.
  • Drawing extra cards instead of reflecting.
  • Comparing your impressions with more experienced readers too harshly.
✓

A Simple Intuitive Reading Method

  1. Read the question carefully.
  2. Look at the card before checking its meaning.
  3. Record the first detail that stands out.
  4. Describe the emotion or story within the image.
  5. Review the traditional card meaning.
  6. Connect your observation with the question.
  7. Check that the complete spread supports your interpretation.
🌱

Confidence Develops Through Practice

You do not need to force an intuitive message from every card.

Some readings will feel clear immediately, while others require more thought.

Confidence grows by observing carefully, recording your impressions and reviewing what you learned later.

Remember

Tarot intuition is not about abandoning traditional meanings or making unsupported predictions. It is the skill of noticing what matters within the card and connecting it carefully to the question, spread position and surrounding cards.

Practise noticing your first impression in a simple reading.
