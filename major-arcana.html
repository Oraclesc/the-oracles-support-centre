The Major Arcana Tarot Cards

The Major Arcana is made up of 22 cards that represent important lessons, turning points, personal growth and powerful life experiences. Together, they tell a symbolic story often known as The Fool's Journey.

0

The Fool

Upright: New beginnings, freedom, curiosity, adventure and taking a leap of faith.

Reversed: Recklessness, hesitation, poor planning or fear of beginning something new.

The Fool represents the beginning of a journey. It encourages openness, trust and a willingness to step into the unknown.

I

The Magician

Upright: Skill, confidence, action, creativity and using available resources.

Reversed: Manipulation, untapped ability, lack of direction or poor use of talents.

The Magician reminds you that you may already have the tools needed to move forward.

II

The High Priestess

Upright: Intuition, inner wisdom, mystery and hidden knowledge.

Reversed: Ignoring intuition, confusion, secrecy or emotional disconnection.

The High Priestess encourages patience and careful listening to your inner voice.

III

The Empress

Upright: Creativity, abundance, nurturing, comfort and growth.

Reversed: Creative blocks, dependence, neglecting yourself or giving too much.

The Empress is connected with care, creation and allowing something meaningful to grow.

IV

The Emperor

Upright: Structure, stability, leadership, authority and discipline.

Reversed: Rigidity, control, stubbornness or misuse of authority.

The Emperor suggests creating order, setting boundaries and taking responsibility.

V

The Hierophant

Upright: Tradition, learning, guidance, shared values and established systems.

Reversed: Challenging tradition, restrictive beliefs or following your own path.

The Hierophant can represent teachers, institutions or beliefs that shape your understanding.

VI

The Lovers

Upright: Connection, harmony, values, attraction and meaningful choices.

Reversed: Disharmony, imbalance, conflict or choices that do not match your values.

The Lovers is not only about romance. It can also represent alignment, trust and important decisions.

VII

The Chariot

Upright: Determination, direction, progress, confidence and self-control.

Reversed: Lack of direction, aggression, obstacles or losing control.

The Chariot encourages focused action and steady movement toward a goal.

VIII

Strength

Upright: Courage, patience, compassion, inner strength and calm confidence.

Reversed: Self-doubt, insecurity, impatience or emotional exhaustion.

Strength represents gentle courage rather than force. It suggests handling challenges with patience and self-control.

IX

The Hermit

Upright: Reflection, solitude, wisdom, guidance and introspection.

Reversed: Isolation, loneliness, withdrawal or avoiding self-reflection.

The Hermit suggests stepping back from outside noise to search for clarity within.

X

Wheel of Fortune

Upright: Change, cycles, opportunity, movement and turning points.

Reversed: Resistance to change, setbacks, repeated patterns or feeling stuck.

The Wheel of Fortune reminds you that situations change and no phase lasts forever.

XI

Justice

Upright: Truth, fairness, accountability, balance and clear decisions.

Reversed: Dishonesty, unfairness, avoidance or refusing responsibility.

Justice asks you to look honestly at actions, consequences and the facts of a situation.

XII

The Hanged Man

Upright: Pause, surrender, patience, release and a new perspective.

Reversed: Delays, resistance, indecision or feeling trapped.

The Hanged Man suggests that progress may require stopping, letting go or seeing things differently.

XIII

Death

Upright: Endings, transformation, release, transition and renewal.

Reversed: Fear of change, resistance, stagnation or holding onto the past.

Death rarely refers to physical death. It usually represents the ending of one phase so another can begin.

XIV

Temperance

Upright: Balance, patience, healing, harmony and moderation.

Reversed: Excess, imbalance, impatience or lack of cooperation.

Temperance encourages a calm, measured approach and the blending of different ideas or energies.

XV

The Devil

Upright: Attachment, temptation, unhealthy habits, materialism and restriction.

Reversed: Breaking free, awareness, release and reclaiming control.

The Devil may highlight patterns, fears or attachments that limit your freedom.

XVI

The Tower

Upright: Sudden change, disruption, revelation and rebuilding.

Reversed: Avoiding change, fear of disruption or delaying an unavoidable truth.

The Tower can feel unsettling, but it often clears away something unstable so a stronger foundation can be built.

XVII

The Star

Upright: Hope, renewal, healing, inspiration and trust.

Reversed: Discouragement, doubt, lack of faith or emotional exhaustion.

The Star brings a sense of calm after difficulty and encourages hope for what comes next.

XVIII

The Moon

Upright: Intuition, uncertainty, dreams, illusion and hidden emotions.

Reversed: Confusion lifting, truth emerging, anxiety or emotional clarity.

The Moon suggests moving carefully when not everything is clear and paying attention to instinct.

XIX

The Sun

Upright: Joy, confidence, success, clarity and vitality.

Reversed: Delayed happiness, low confidence, unrealistic expectations or temporary setbacks.

The Sun is associated with warmth, openness and a clearer understanding of the path ahead.

XX

Judgement

Upright: Awakening, reflection, renewal, accountability and important decisions.

Reversed: Self-doubt, avoidance, harsh judgement or refusing to learn from the past.

Judgement encourages honest reflection and the chance to move forward with greater awareness.

XXI

The World

Upright: Completion, achievement, fulfilment, wholeness and successful endings.

Reversed: Unfinished business, delays, lack of closure or a goal still out of reach.

The World represents the completion of a cycle and the understanding gained through the journey.

The Fool's Journey

The Major Arcana can be viewed as a symbolic journey from innocence and possibility in The Fool to completion and understanding in The World. Each card represents a stage of growth, challenge or discovery along the way.
