The Minor Arcana Tarot Cards

The Minor Arcana contains 56 cards connected with everyday experiences, emotions, decisions, relationships, work and personal challenges. These cards are divided into four suits: Cups, Pentacles, Swords and Wands.

✦

What Is the Minor Arcana?

While the Major Arcana often represents larger life lessons and important turning points, the Minor Arcana usually focuses on the situations and emotions that appear in daily life.

A Minor Arcana card might describe a conversation, a practical concern, a relationship dynamic, a creative idea or a decision that needs attention.

The Minor Arcana is made up of four suits. Each suit contains 14 cards: Ace through Ten, followed by the Page, Knight, Queen and King.

♡

Cups

Element: Water

Main themes: Emotions, relationships, intuition, love, compassion and connection.

Cups are closely connected with the emotional world. They can represent how you feel, how you connect with others and how openly you express affection, empathy or vulnerability.

When Cups appear frequently in a reading, emotional matters may be especially important. The reading could involve relationships, healing, imagination or the need to listen to your intuition.

Difficult Cups cards may point towards disappointment, emotional distance, unrealistic expectations or feelings that have not been fully understood.

Pentacles

Element: Earth

Main themes: Work, money, health, stability, home, skills and practical matters.

Pentacles represent the physical and practical parts of life. They often appear in readings about employment, finances, possessions, long-term plans or personal security.

This suit can also represent patience and gradual progress. Pentacles often remind you that strong results take time, effort and consistent care.

Challenging Pentacles cards may highlight financial stress, insecurity, overwork, poor planning or a need to take better care of your physical wellbeing.

Swords

Element: Air

Main themes: Thoughts, communication, truth, decisions, conflict and mental clarity.

Swords are connected with the mind. They often describe your thoughts, beliefs, conversations and the way you approach difficult decisions.

Swords can represent intelligence, honesty and clear communication. They can also show tension, anxiety, arguments or the consequences of harsh words.

When many Swords appear in a reading, you may be overthinking a situation or facing an important truth that needs to be acknowledged.

Explore the Suit of Swords
♨

Wands

Element: Fire

Main themes: Energy, passion, ambition, creativity, action and personal growth.

Wands represent motivation and movement. They often appear when you are beginning a project, pursuing a goal or feeling inspired to make a change.

This suit can describe confidence, leadership, enthusiasm and the courage to act on an idea.

Difficult Wands cards may suggest frustration, burnout, delays, scattered energy or conflict caused by competing ambitions.

The Meaning of the Numbers

Every suit follows a journey from Ace to Ten. Understanding the general meaning of each number can make Minor Arcana cards easier to interpret.

Aces

Aces represent beginnings, opportunities and the first spark of potential. The suit reveals what kind of new energy is becoming available.

Twos

Twos often represent balance, choices, partnerships or two different possibilities.

Threes

Threes are connected with growth, development, creativity and cooperation.

Fours

Fours often represent stability, structure, security or the need for rest.

Fives

Fives frequently indicate change, conflict, disruption or an uncomfortable challenge.

Sixes

Sixes can represent movement, harmony, support, recovery or progress after difficulty.

Sevens

Sevens often involve reflection, assessment, patience or deciding how to continue.

Eights

Eights are associated with movement, effort, mastery, progress or restriction.

Nines

Nines often represent independence, experience and reaching the final stage before completion.

Tens

Tens represent completion, results and the ending of a cycle. They may also show what happens when the energy of a suit reaches its fullest point.

♙

The Court Cards

Each Minor Arcana suit contains four court cards: the Page, Knight, Queen and King.

Court cards can represent people, personality traits, approaches to a situation or parts of your own character.

Pages

Pages often represent curiosity, learning, messages, fresh ideas and the beginning of development.

Knights

Knights represent movement, action and the active pursuit of the suit's energy. They can also show extremes or impulsive behaviour.

Queens

Queens often represent maturity, inner strength, understanding and the inward expression of their suit.

Kings

Kings represent leadership, experience, authority and the confident outward expression of their suit.

↕

Reversed Minor Arcana Cards

A reversed card appears upside down. Reversed Minor Arcana cards do not always mean the opposite of their upright interpretation.

A reversal may suggest that the card's energy is blocked, delayed, hidden, weakened or expressed in an unhealthy way.

For example, an upright Ace of Wands can represent inspiration and a new opportunity. Reversed, it might suggest low motivation, delays or an idea that has not yet been developed.

Beginners can choose to keep every card upright while learning. Reversals can be introduced later when you feel more confident.

✧

How to Read Several Minor Arcana Cards Together

When several Minor Arcana cards appear in one reading, look for patterns across the suits, numbers and images.

Ask yourself:

  • Is one suit appearing more often than the others?
  • Are the cards mostly low numbers or high numbers?
  • Are several court cards present?
  • Do the images feel calm, active, emotional or tense?
  • Are the cards supporting each other or showing conflicting influences?

A reading filled with Cups may focus strongly on emotions, while many Swords may show mental stress or an important conversation.

Several Aces may indicate new beginnings. Several Tens may suggest that an important cycle is ending.

Major Arcana and Minor Arcana Together

Most tarot readings include a mixture of Major and Minor Arcana cards.

Major Arcana cards often show the larger lesson or turning point, while Minor Arcana cards provide more detail about how that lesson appears in daily life.

For example, The Chariot may show determination and forward movement, while the Eight of Pentacles may explain that progress depends on practice, discipline and developing your skills.

When several Major Arcana cards appear, the reading may involve an especially meaningful period of change. When Minor Arcana cards dominate, the situation may be more connected with practical decisions and everyday experiences.

A Simple Way to Remember the Four Suits

Cups feel, Pentacles build, Swords think and Wands act.

This simple pattern can help you understand the main energy of a card before exploring its full meaning.
