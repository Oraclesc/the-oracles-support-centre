Keeping a Tarot Journal

One of the fastest ways to improve your tarot reading skills is to keep a journal. Recording your readings helps you notice patterns, build confidence and understand how the meanings of the cards develop through real experience rather than simple memorisation.

📖

What Is a Tarot Journal?

A tarot journal is simply a place where you record your readings, thoughts and observations.

It does not need to be beautifully decorated or perfectly organised.

Its purpose is to help you learn from every reading you perform.

🌱

Why Keep a Tarot Journal?

Many beginners feel that they are forgetting card meanings or making slow progress.

A journal shows how much you have actually learned over time.

Looking back at earlier readings often reveals that your interpretations become clearer and more detailed with regular practice.

✍

Paper or Digital?

There is no right or wrong choice.

Some people enjoy writing in a notebook because it encourages slower, more thoughtful reflection.

Others prefer digital documents, spreadsheets or note-taking apps that allow easy searching and organisation.

Choose the method that you are most likely to keep using consistently.

📅

Always Record the Date

Every journal entry should begin with the date.

This allows you to return weeks or months later and see how your understanding has developed.

It also helps you recognise how your readings relate to real events over time.

❓

Write Down Your Question

Many people remember the cards but forget the exact question they asked.

Without the original question, it becomes much harder to judge whether the reading was helpful.

Always record your question exactly as you asked it.

🃏

Record Every Card

Write down each card in the order it appeared.

If you use reversals, note whether each card was upright or reversed.

If you use a spread, also record the position of every card.

💭

Write Your First Impression

Before checking any guidebook, write down what you noticed immediately.

What emotions did the card create?

Which symbols stood out?

What story did the artwork seem to tell?

Your first impression is often just as valuable as the traditional meaning.

📚

Compare With Traditional Meanings

After recording your own thoughts, compare them with a trusted guidebook.

Notice where your observations agree with traditional meanings and where they differ.

This develops both your knowledge and your personal understanding of the cards.

😊

Record Your Feelings

How were you feeling before the reading?

Were you calm, worried, excited or uncertain?

Recording your emotional state can help explain why certain cards stood out more strongly than others.

🔄

Notice Recurring Cards

Certain cards may appear repeatedly over several weeks.

Rather than seeing this as strange, consider what lesson or theme those cards may be encouraging you to explore.

A journal makes these recurring patterns much easier to spot.

📝

Example Journal Entry

Your journal entries do not need to be long. Even a few lines can become valuable references later.

A simple entry might look like this:

  • Date: 19 July 2026
  • Question: What should I focus on this week?
  • Spread: One Card
  • Card: The Hermit
  • First Impression: Slow down and spend time thinking before acting.
  • Guidebook: Reflection, wisdom and personal growth.
  • Outcome: Looking back, taking extra time before making a decision was helpful.
📈

Track Your Progress

Every few weeks, read through older journal entries.

You will often notice that meanings which once felt confusing now seem obvious.

This is one of the clearest signs that your understanding of tarot is developing.

👁

Look for Patterns

A journal makes it much easier to recognise repeating themes.

You may notice the same suit appearing repeatedly, the same Major Arcana card returning over several months or certain cards appearing whenever you ask about a particular area of your life.

These patterns are often far more valuable than any single reading.

💡

Develop Your Own Meanings

Traditional meanings provide an excellent foundation, but over time you may discover personal connections with certain cards.

Perhaps The Star always appears before positive opportunities in your own life, or The Chariot often reminds you to stay disciplined.

Recording these observations helps create a reading style that is personal while still respecting the traditional symbolism.

⚠

Common Journaling Mistakes

  • Only recording the cards and nothing else.
  • Forgetting to write the original question.
  • Never reviewing previous entries.
  • Expecting every reading to be completely accurate.
  • Comparing your journal to somebody else's.
  • Stopping after only a few entries.

Your journal is a learning tool, not a test.

⏳

Be Patient With Yourself

Some journal entries will seem insightful immediately.

Others may only make sense weeks or months later.

That is completely normal. Tarot is a skill developed through observation, reflection and experience.

✨

A Habit Worth Building

You do not need to journal every reading forever.

However, recording your readings while learning tarot can dramatically improve both your confidence and your understanding of the cards.

Even five minutes of journaling after each reading can provide insights that would otherwise be forgotten.

Remember

Your tarot journal does not need to be perfect. Its purpose is simply to help you notice patterns, reflect on your readings and become a more confident reader over time.
